DES Cipher (Data Encryption Standard)

DES is a symmetric block cipher, encrypting data in 64-bit blocks using a 56-bit key. Developed in the 1970s, it was once a widely used standard but is now considered insecure for most applications due to its relatively short key length, making it vulnerable to brute-force attacks. However, it's still foundational for understanding cryptography.
